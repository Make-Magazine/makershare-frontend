SUPPORTING OPEN INNOVATION

Make: + Intel

We Are All Makers

In 2016, Make: and Intel came together with a shared vision: creating a global Maker community for open innovation. Each felt the Maker community needed a place where Makers could share their stories, their projects and their knowledge, a digital destination where they could connect in the same way they do at Maker Faires around the world.

To this end, Make: and Intel co-created Maker Share.

With the launch of Make: magazine in 2005, Dale Dougherty and his team provided the catalyst for a tech-influenced DIY community that has come to be identified as the Maker Movement.

At the heart of the Maker Movement is the realization that making is something that many of us enjoy. As people learn to become makers and develop projects, they become innovators, as makers of change.

Here on Maker Share, makers can document their participation in the growing Maker movement, including spotlighting their presence at Maker Faires and engagement in Intel Maker Outreach activities, such as challenges and community missions.

A central tool for achieving this are personal Maker Portfolios that provide a curated view of an individual Maker’s projects and abilities.

The goal for me for Maker Share is to do some of the magic that we do at Maker Faire online, where almost anybody has access to it.
Dale Dougherty, Make: Founder and CEO

Maker Portfolios are especially relevant in an educational setting; many educational institutions are beginning to accept learning portfolios as another measure, like grades and test results, of a student’s aptitude. As a record of a student’s hands-on learning – both the final product and the creative process – Maker Portfolios belong in any setting where a student presents their accomplishment.

Maker Share also provides a learning center that will help makers of different backgrounds develop new skills and learn to incorporate new technologies into their builds. Drawing on the Make: catalog of books, thousands of published projects and produced videos as well as Intel learning support, Maker Share will create learning paths that help beginners get started and advanced makers refine and validate their skills.

The skills you learn through making is what is going to make you successful in innovating in the Fourth Industrial Revolution.
Rajiv Mongia, Director Maker Experience and Outreach at Intel

What will you make?

Make: and Intel share a vision of a community of makers, fueled by a passion to create, who will come together online to celebrate their successes…and their failures. Maker Share will foster their collaboration and connect them to each other and to opportunities; by so doing, we are creating a hub for open innovation.
