Dos and Don’ts

TO MAKE SURE THIS IS A PLACE FOR EVERYONE

The following guidelines are intended to keep Maker Share a friendly, supportive and open community where makers of all kinds are welcome and encouraged to participate -- regardless of age, gender, race, ethnicity, religion, sexual orientation or skill level.

DO Tell Your Story

Maker Share encourages you to share what you made, how you made it and why you made it. You are creating a valuable record of your own work and your own process of creating it. Your projects reflect your interests and your talents, providing evidence of a maker’s knowledge and expertise. Please also identify the resources you used such as tools and materials so that others are able to re-create your project.

DO Share Credit

Projects can be the result of the collaborating with other people. Be sure to provide credit to the people who helped on the project. Don’t claim ownership of work you did not create or use photos belonging to others without obtaining permission.

DO Share Your Projects Openly

When you share a project on Maker Share, you can decide how much you want to reveal about the project and the development process. The more you share the more others are likely to be interested in your project and provide you with meaningful feedback. We don’t own the content you post here. Any content, including images, sounds, and sample code, that you post, upload, provide, submit, share, publish, distribute or make available on Maker Share is automatically licensed under the Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 4.0 license. This allows others to view, re-use and remix all of this content. This license requires you to: make sure you have any necessary permissions to distribute some piece of content, for example, a photo attribute any re-used material to the original author. Likewise, know that we’re distributing the content from you and your fellow makers “as is.” We’re not responsible for its accuracy or for what happens when you use it!

DO Contribute to the Community

By sharing your project(s), you become a member who contributes to the maker community on Maker Share. What makes community possible is our shared values: openness, resourcefulness, ingenuity, generosity, creativity, resilience and self-directedness, to mention a few. You can support the members of this community by engaging other members and appreciating their contributions.

DO Provide Constructive Feedback

As a member of this website, you have the opportunity to interact with makers and respond to the projects that have been shared. Please seek to provide constructive feedback that shows respect for makers and their efforts but also helps them understand their project in a new way. In short, be helpful and kind. Our goal is to foster a supportive community where any maker of any level of expertise can feel comfortable to participate.

DO Put Safety First

As makers, we have an obligation to work safely and protect ourselves as well as others and the tools we use. Please consider how you can ensure that others follow safety guidelines when they replicate your project using powerful tools. Likewise, when replicating someone else’s project, make sure you understand what you are doing before you undertake the project so that you are NOT a danger to yourself or others.

DON’T Share Offensive Material

Maker Share is not the place for any content that is Not Safe For Work. Young Makers hang out here, so just don’t. Also, please be considerate in your use of language.

DON’T Violate Anyone’s Privacy

Members of the community on this site are not anonymous. Real names and geographical data (when selected) are used on Maker Share, with the goal of encouraging makers to connect both online and off. However, do not make public your or anyone else’s personal contact information such as physical address, email address, or phone number, even if you believe you have the other person’s permission.

DON’T Spam the Community

It is not okay to post exclusively commercial messages or the same message repeatedly across different forums for any purpose.

DON’T Break Maker Share

This website is used by many people around the world and individuals interfering with the site’s normal functions will affect everyone's ability to use this website properly.

TELL US

Help us enforce these guidelines by reporting a user profile, a comment or a project that you suspect violates our Community Guidelines or Terms of Use. You only need to report an item once; our team reviews reported con- tent every day and will follow up as necessary to resolve the issue. Our Community Managers will always try to resolve any issues with users first, but reserve the right to remove without notice content that violates these guidelines or anyone’s intellectual property or personal rights. Com- munity Managers also reserve the right to delete a user’s account, as a last resort, if someone repeatedly flouts these guidelines. Have another suggestion for our Community Guidelines?
