WHY?

Create a Maker Portfolio

A portfolio tells the stories behind your projects and helps you find new opportunities in the community. Artists, designers, photographers use portfolios to demonstrate their skills to schools, jobs and clients. Online code repositories like Github lets software engineers showcase their work in a similar fashion. Portfolios are useful because they focus not on personal connections, or paper credentials, or unverified claims...but show what the owner has really accomplished. A Maker Portfolio is the same. Your Maker Portfolio also connects the dots in your creative process from Point A (I had this idea) to Point B (I made this thing) to Point X (here’s what I learned).

CREATE. CONNECT. LEARN.

Makers learn by doing. The sharing of projects that harness creative insight, technical skill, and a ‘hands-on’ approach is critical to Maker culture.

Your Maker Portfolio is a curated representation of your interests, skills, processes, and projects.

It shows growth and progress and is a rich source of insight into your ability and development.

Most importantly, it initiates conversations within the Maker community, connecting you to fellow makers but also with potential employers, commissions, consulting, educational and other opportunities.

For student Makers, a portfolio reflects a depth of understanding not captured by the traditional grading process.

A Maker Portfolio demonstrates to parents, teachers, peers and even school admissions officials knowledge, technical skills and “soft” skills like project management and the ability to articulate an idea.

WHAT DO YOU MEAN BY SHOW & TELL?

Your portfolio tells your story as a maker.

We call Maker Faire the Greatest Show & Tell on Earth because makers come to show their work and talk about their projects...that’s how they get to know each other. You can do the same on Maker Share.

One of the most important aspects of a portfolio is that it shows others how you think - through thumbnails, sketchbooks, snapshots, bills of materials, helpful tips, and even your notes.

You can show a final product and explain how you made it and that is a valuable contribution to the Maker community. It is even richer to also share your experience of developing that project.

By answering these simple questions in your Project Story -- or better yet your Show & Tell video! -- you bring your whole self to the work and help people relate.

What is your project? How did you make it and why? What inspired you? Who helped you? What went right? What went wrong? What did you learn from this project?

WHAT GOES INTO YOUR PORTFOLIO?

In developing a Maker Portfolio, the process Is the product.

For an artist or photographer, the focus is usually on the finished product. Many Makers also create visually stunning artifacts or projects that can be replicated or hacked by others. Your Maker Portfolio supports that by including a robust How-to section.

For some, however, a photo of the finished product may be less interesting than the Behind the Scenes story. Even “failed” projects can belong in a Portfolio if they explain what you learned from your failure.

Bottom line: Your Maker Portfolio is a curated collection of your work.

1. SELECT. Choose the projects you’re most proud of, and/or which you think represent your best or most interesting work...even if the final product didn’t come out as planned. Do you want people to see you as a poly-Maker or would you prefer your portfolio focused on just one or two things, to really highlight these skills and abilities?

2. MIX IT UP. Make sure you have some variety, if not in the type of project, then in how you showcase it visually and how you tell the story so your portfolio itself looks interesting and varied.

3. REVIEW, ADD AND EDIT. Keep your portfolio fresh by reviewing it and editing frequently.

4. SHARE. We’ve given you a link for your portfolio that clearly states what it is, where it lives, and -- most importantly -- that it’s yours. Link to it from your other social profiles, include it in any websites or blogs you keep about your work and use it to connect to other Makers in the community.
